About

I've never been
just one thing.

Linguist. Dancer. Creator. Director. Writer. The list exists — but it was never the point.

The point is the thread underneath all of it: how people become more fully themselves. How language shapes the way we see who we are. How movement says what words can't. How a story, told honestly, can crack something open in someone who didn't know they needed it.

I studied Critical Discourse Analysis — which is a formal way of saying I was trained to read what's between the lines. I direct dance productions with TEQUILA, where every performance is built from concept to final frame. I create content rooted in growth, honesty, and the quiet courage it takes to stop shrinking.

I work with people who were told to pick one thing — and never quite believed they should have to.

A little more of me

Beyond the
resume.

I've been part of a community called TEQUILA — a dance group that started as a place to move and became a place to belong. We don't just rehearse. We show up for each other, we make things together, and somewhere between the music and the movement, we have a lot of fun.

Teaching has taken me across many different rooms — from kindergarten all the way to university level. English, mostly. But what I've really been teaching, underneath all of it, is this: that you are more capable than you think. Whether it's a five-year-old sounding out their first words or a university student finding their voice before a crowd — I show up for the ones trying to build their confidence and figure out their path.

I'll be honest — I am not a math person. At all. Some things just aren't for everyone, and I've made my peace with that.

Outside of work, I fill my days the way most humans do: cooking something (sometimes it turns out well), cleaning (it's meditative, truly), and slow hangouts with friends where the time just disappears. Nothing complicated. Just good, quiet, real life.

Despite the turbulent mind — and it is turbulent, often — the only strategy that has ever actually worked is this: just go with it. Find the thread. Keep moving.
